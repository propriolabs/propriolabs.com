FAQ

How do I download Tennis Sense?

You can download Tennis Sense on the Google Play Store. You'll need an Android phone and Android Wear. We're working on a version for the Apple Watch, stay tuned!

What is Tennis Sense and what does it do?

Tennis Sense is an activity tracker and tennis sensor app for your smartwatch. It tracks your performance while you play tennis. Specifically, Tennis Sense uses motion sensors to track your forehands, backhands, serves, and more. With the smartwatch's built-in microphone, Tennis Sense tracks the match score.

How does Tennis Sense help me improve my game?

Tennis Sense generates statistics and visualizations every time you play tennis. Tennis Sense allows you to explore details about your tennis game that you have never had access to before. You'll start to answer questions like: What proportion of my strokes are forehands vs. backhands? How often am I double faulting? How long are my rallies? How do any of these questions change as I get tired? How do any of these questions change depending on whom I play against. With detailed data-driven insights into your game, you will be more aware on the court of your strengths and weaknesses.

What other features are coming for Tennis Sense?

We are building in a social component, so you can share stats with friends, teammates, and your tennis coach. In the near future, we'll be adding local positioning analytics, so you know where on the court you hit your shot. Stay tuned!

What watches does Tennis Sense support?

Tennis Sense is compatible with Android Wear devices with accelerometers, gyroscopes, and microphones. The majority of Tennis Sense users have either a Sony SmartWatch 3 or a Moto 360 Sport. The 1st generation Moto 360 is not supported because Motorola disabled the gyroscope with a software patch and is no longer maintaining it. We are working on an Apple Watch version, which will be ready in the near future.
